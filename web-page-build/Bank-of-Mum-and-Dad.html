Banking on Mum and Dad?

Soaring house prices are making it harder and harder for the next generation to get a stable home of their own.

Many parents can no longer afford to help their children out with a deposit.

And those who can have to dig into their savings and pension pots to do so - forking out on average £23,000. Yet politicians still refuse to act.

This can't go on. It's right that young people work hard and save up for their first home, but politicians should meet them halfway.

How much would you have to borrow from the Bank of Mum and Dad for a deposit where you live?
